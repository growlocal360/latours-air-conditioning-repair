What Qualifies as an AC Emergency?

An AC emergency is any situation where the loss of cooling poses a risk to health, safety, or property:

  • Complete AC failure during extreme heat
  • AC stopped working with elderly, infants, or medically vulnerable family members at home
  • AC stopped working with pets that can't be relocated
  • Burning smell or smoke from your AC system
  • AC making loud, unusual noises before stopping
  • Water flooding from your indoor AC unit
  • AC tripping breaker repeatedly

While You Wait for Emergency Service

If your AC has failed and you're waiting for our technician, here are some tips to stay safe and cool:

  • Check your thermostat: Make sure it's set to "cool" and the temperature is set below current room temperature
  • Check your breaker: Try resetting the AC breaker if it's tripped
  • Check your filter: A severely clogged filter can cause system shutdown
  • Close blinds and curtains: Block out sunlight to reduce heat gain
  • Use fans: Ceiling and portable fans help circulate air
  • Stay hydrated: Drink plenty of water, especially if you're elderly or have health conditions
  • Relocate if necessary: If temperatures become dangerous, move to a cooler location

Prevent AC Emergencies

The best emergency is the one that never happens. Regular maintenance helps prevent unexpected breakdowns:

  • Schedule annual AC tune-ups before summer
  • Change your air filter monthly during heavy use
  • Keep outdoor unit clear of debris
  • Address strange noises or performance issues promptly
  • Consider a maintenance plan for priority service
