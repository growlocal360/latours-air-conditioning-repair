Heat Pump Installation in Lake Charles, LA

Energy-efficient heating and cooling in one system. Professional installation with proper sizing, quality equipment, and expert workmanship.

A heat pump is the ideal HVAC solution for Lake Charles homes. These energy-efficient systems provide both heating and cooling, operating at peak efficiency in Louisiana's mild climate. Latour's HVAC provides professional heat pump installation with proper sizing, quality equipment, and expert workmanship.

Unlike traditional systems that generate heat, heat pumps transfer heat—moving it outside in summer and inside in winter. This makes them 2-3 times more efficient than conventional heating systems, saving you money every month on energy bills.

Why Heat Pumps Are Perfect for Lake Charles

Heat pumps work most efficiently when outdoor temperatures stay above freezing—and that describes Louisiana's climate perfectly. Here's why heat pumps excel in our area:

  • Mild Winters: Lake Charles rarely sees temperatures below 30°F, the range where heat pumps operate most efficiently
  • Year-Round Use: Get both heating and cooling from a single system
  • High Efficiency: SEER ratings up to 20+ for cooling and HSPF ratings of 10+ for heating
  • Lower Operating Costs: Can reduce heating costs by 30-50% compared to electric resistance heating
  • Even Comfort: Heat pumps provide consistent temperatures without the hot/cold swings of furnaces

Types of Heat Pumps We Install

Air Source Heat Pumps

The most common and cost-effective option. Air source heat pumps extract heat from outdoor air (even in cold weather) and transfer it inside. Modern units work efficiently down to temperatures well below what Lake Charles typically experiences.

Ductless Mini Split Heat Pumps

Perfect for room additions, homes without ductwork, or creating separate comfort zones. Each indoor unit is controlled independently, providing efficient zone control.

Dual Fuel Systems

Combines a heat pump with a gas furnace backup. The heat pump handles most heating needs efficiently, while the furnace kicks in during the coldest days. Provides the best of both worlds.

Heat Pump vs. Traditional AC + Furnace

Wondering if a heat pump is right for you? Here's how they compare:

  • Installation Cost: Heat pumps cost slightly more upfront but eliminate the need for a separate heating system
  • Operating Cost: Heat pumps are significantly cheaper to operate, especially for heating
  • Efficiency: Heat pumps can be 2-3x more efficient than gas furnaces for heating
  • Lifespan: Both systems last 15-20 years with proper maintenance
  • Comfort: Heat pumps provide more even temperatures; furnaces deliver warmer air
