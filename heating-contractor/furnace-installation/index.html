Furnace Installation in Lake Charles, LA

Professional gas and electric furnace installation. Proper sizing, quality equipment, and expert workmanship for reliable winter heating.

Need a new furnace installation in Lake Charles? Latour's HVAC installs high-efficiency gas and electric furnaces designed for reliable heating during Louisiana winters. We help you choose the right furnace for your home, ensure proper sizing, and provide expert installation backed by manufacturer warranties.

While heat pumps are popular in our mild climate, many Lake Charles homeowners prefer the warmth of a gas furnace. Gas furnaces deliver hotter air (110-140°F) compared to heat pumps (85-95°F), which some people find more comfortable during cold snaps.

Types of Furnaces We Install

Gas Furnaces

The most popular choice for Lake Charles homes with natural gas service. Modern gas furnaces achieve efficiency ratings of 80-98% AFUE (Annual Fuel Utilization Efficiency).

  • Single-Stage: Basic operation, lower cost, reliable performance
  • Two-Stage: Runs at low capacity most of the time for better comfort and efficiency
  • Variable-Speed/Modulating: Precise temperature control, highest efficiency, quietest operation

Electric Furnaces

A good option for homes without gas service. Electric furnaces have lower installation costs but higher operating costs than gas.

Dual Fuel Systems

Combines a heat pump with a gas furnace backup. The heat pump handles mild weather efficiently, while the furnace provides powerful heating during the coldest days.

Signs You Need a New Furnace

  • Furnace is 15-20 years old
  • Frequent repairs becoming costly
  • Energy bills increasing significantly
  • Uneven heating throughout your home
  • Furnace makes unusual noises
  • Yellow or flickering pilot light (indicates combustion issues)
  • Visible rust or cracks in the unit

Furnace vs. Heat Pump: Which Is Right for You?

Both options work well in Lake Charles. Here's a quick comparison:

  • Gas Furnace: Lower operating cost if gas is cheap, delivers warmer air, works well in existing ducted homes
  • Heat Pump: Higher efficiency, provides both heating and cooling, lower carbon footprint
  • Dual Fuel: Best of both worlds—efficiency plus backup power

We'll help you evaluate your options based on your home, energy costs, and comfort preferences.
