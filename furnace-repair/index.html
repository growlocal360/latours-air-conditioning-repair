Common Furnace Problems We Repair

Our technicians are experienced with all types of furnace issues, from simple fixes to complex repairs:

  • Furnace Not Heating: The most common complaint—we identify whether it's a thermostat issue, pilot light problem, ignition failure, or more serious component failure.
  • Furnace Blowing Cold Air: Often caused by thermostat settings, clogged filters, or pilot/ignition issues. We diagnose and fix the root cause.
  • Strange Noises: Grinding, squealing, banging, or rumbling sounds indicate specific problems that we can identify and repair.
  • Frequent Cycling: If your furnace turns on and off too frequently, it could be a thermostat, airflow, or flame sensor issue.
  • Pilot Light Problems: Pilot lights that won't stay lit often indicate thermocouple issues or gas supply problems.
  • Ignitor Failure: Modern furnaces use electronic ignition—we repair and replace faulty ignitors.
  • Blower Motor Issues: Problems with the blower motor affect airflow and heating performance.
  • Gas Valve Problems: Issues with the gas valve can prevent proper fuel delivery to the burners.

Signs Your Furnace Needs Repair

Don't wait for a complete breakdown. Call for furnace repair if you notice:

  • Uneven heating or cold spots in your home
  • Yellow or flickering pilot light (should be blue)
  • Unusual smells when the furnace runs
  • Increased energy bills without explanation
  • Excessive dust or dry air
  • Furnace running constantly without reaching set temperature
  • Any carbon monoxide detector alerts

Furnace Repair vs. Replacement

We're honest about when repair makes sense and when replacement is the better investment. Generally, consider replacement if:

  • Your furnace is 15-20 years old
  • Repair costs exceed 50% of replacement cost
  • You're experiencing frequent breakdowns
  • Energy bills keep rising despite maintenance
  • The heat exchanger is cracked (safety issue)

When replacement is recommended, we provide honest options and expert installation of energy-efficient systems.
